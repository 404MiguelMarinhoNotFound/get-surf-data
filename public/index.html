Reading the score

The score blends wave height, period, wind, swell direction, and tide when available into one number — 0 to 10. Here's what each colour actually means on the water.

🔥 7.5 and above — Firing

Everything's lined up at once. Good swell height, clean period, wind cooperating — or better yet, light offshore grooming the face. Think 1.2–1.8m at 12–14 seconds with cross-shore or offshore wind. You'll get proper faces, some punch on the sets, and waves that reward commitment. These sessions don't happen every week. When you see gold, move things around and go.

🤙 6.2 to 7.4 — Solid

Definitely worth paddling out. Not perfect, but conditions are working with you rather than against you. Maybe a touch of texture from the wind, or the swell direction's not dialed in perfectly, but you'll get good rides and leave satisfied. The kind of session you don't regret — solid enough to bring a friend.

5.0 to 6.1 — Rideable

Surfable. You'll catch waves and have an okay time, especially if you're already nearby. Might be smaller and cleaner, or a bit bigger but textured — works either way, just not exciting. Good for a training paddle or keeping the stoke alive between swells. Skip it if you're driving more than 20 minutes.

4.4 to 4.9 — Barely

On the edge of worth it. Something's off — maybe too small, wind starting to mess things up, or the swell's hitting the break at a bad angle. You'd probably still paddle out if you were already there, but you wouldn't plan a session around it. If the week looks flat overall it might be your best shot, otherwise hold out.

Below 4.4 — Weak

Leave it. The models are pointing to flat, blown out, or just wrong conditions. Could be too small to bother, strong onshore wind turning it to chop, or the tide shutting down the shape entirely. No shame in a rest day — check back tomorrow or look further in the week.
